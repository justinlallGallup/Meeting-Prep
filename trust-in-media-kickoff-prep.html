Facilitation Approach & Networking Assignments

Key Facilitation Principles (from Justin's email)

Create space — don't fill it

In the discussion sections, our role is mainly to create space for the clients to react, shape, and brainstorm. Tee up the questions, offer brief framing where helpful, and then let USC, TIME, and McKinsey do most of the talking. This ensures the conversation reflects their priorities and gives us the input we need.

Save the brainstorming for later

Don't go too far into brainstorming on our end during the kickoff. We'll have other working sessions to dig in together, pressure-test ideas, and do active ideation. For this meeting: strong facilitation, clear framing, room for client input.

Internal roles during discussion sections

JL = Moderator (frames each question, manages flow) · MK = Note-taker · Kristjan = Timekeeper

Gallup Trust in Media Historical Data

Trust in Media at New Low of 28% in U.S. — Key reference for the "why now" framing

Key Background Context

In the 1970s, nearly three out of four American adults had at least a fair amount of trust in mass media. Today, only 28% of Americans report trusting the media. The Trust in Media Initiative will be a long-term, semi-annual national survey of 3,500 U.S. adults measuring public trust in the media, tracking changes over time, and identifying the factors that shape trust across different groups of Americans. This is a collaborative initiative between Gallup, USC Annenberg, TIME, and McKinsey.
